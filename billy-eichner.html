Billy Eichner: Actor, Comedian, Age, Net Worth !

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, March 10, 2024

Billy Eichner is an American actor, comedian, producer, and screenwriter. He is the star, creator, and executive producer of Funny Or Die’s Billy on the Street, a comedy game show aired on truTV. In 2013, the show earned Billy a nomination for the Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Game Show Host.

He is also popular for playing Mr. Ambrose the Librarian on the animated TV series Bob Burgers, Craig Middlebrooks on the sitcom Parks and Recreation, and Timon in the 2019 remake of The Lion King.

Bros Star Billy Eichner Shared Thoughts On Twitter For Box Office Opening

Billy Eichner, the writer and producer who also has starred in “Bros,” shared his thoughts on his Twitter thread regarding the film’s meager box office opening. He said he is very proud of the film and wrote, “Straight People, especially in certain parts of the country, just didn’t show up for Bros. And that’s disappointing, but it is what it is.”

“Bros” is an LGBTQ romantic comedy and the first gay film by Universal Pictures. The expected opening range was $8-10 million. But the film took in just $4.8 million, playing in 3,350 locations this weekend. Even with great reviews, excellent Rotten Tomatoes scores, and an A CinemaScore, It was fourth at the box office.

The film was produced for a modest $22M, and Universal spent another $30M on advertising and promotion. 

Bros played well in big cities, with its best numbers coming from New York, Los Angeles, and San Francisco. It did less well elsewhere, though, especially in the South. People who saw the comedy this weekend enjoyed it.

Billy Eichner Career:

Billy rose to fame when he hosted and was also the Creation Nation: A Live Talk Show writer. It was a critically acclaimed stage show in NY. He landed a Bravo (an American TV network) web series titled My Life on the Z-List: Jen’s Vlog. Further, Billy was a co-host on Joan Rivers’ Straight Talk, of Bravo television pilot, where Rivers discussed topics with four gay men. He also appeared as a special correspondent in original video shorts on Conan.    

In 2011, Billy starred in the show Billy on the Street. The show was initially aired on Fuse, then moved to truTV. It was created within Funny or Die (a comedy video website), and currently, it’s available on HBO Max and TruTV. It had short-form episodes across digital platforms associated with Lyft. In it, Billy goes around the streets of New York City, having them compete in trivia games and asking people random questions. Often many celebrities go around the streets with him or compete in games.

On 5th August 2013, Billy starred as a guest in the sixth season of Parks and Recreation. On 10th October 2013, the first episode was aired, and he became a series regular in episode four of the show’s seventh season.

In 2014, during the 66th Primetime Emmy Awards show, Billy appeared with host Seth Meyers interviewing strangers randomly on the streets of New York. Billy starred in Difficult People, the Hulu original series, and Julie Klausner. This series was executive produced by Amy Poehler. It aired on Hulu in August 2015 and was initially set up for USA Network. 

On 20th May 2016, Neighbors 2: Sorority Rising was released, in which Billy appeared. Further, In 2017, he conjoins the cast of American Horror Story for recurring roles in both the seventh and eighth seasons.

In 2019, Billy provided the voice of Timon in The Lion King, a remarkable Disney live-action by director Jon Favreau. In July 2020, Billy announced that he and Tom McNulty are working on a film biography of Paul Lynde, a fellow Northwestern University alum, called Man in the Box, and Billy portraying Lynde. 

In March 2021, Billy announced that he was currently starring and writing in the film Bros; the story revolves around two gay men who decide to settle down with each other but have some commitment issues. Universal Pictures produced this film, and it is the first adult-oriented LGBT movie ever produced by a mainstream film studio. Later in August, Amazon Studios announced they had bought the rights to develop the film Ex-Husbands, starring Billy and co-written by him and Paul Rudnick.
